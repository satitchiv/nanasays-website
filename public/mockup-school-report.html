Cheltenham Ladies' College

A large, academically ambitious single-sex boarding school in a handsome Regency town — founded 1854, still run under its original Royal Charter, unapologetically aimed at girls who will take university and public life seriously.

The one-minute verdict

Cheltenham Ladies' College is financially healthy, inspected well, and academically high-performing. It will suit a bright, self-reliant girl who is comfortable in a large, boarding-majority community. It will be a harder fit for a quieter child who needs a small-school feel or a family who needs a bursary to join at age 13 or 14 — those windows are closed. Nothing in the public record raises a safeguarding concern. There is one open finding from the 2022 inspection about whether pupils feel confident raising issues — worth probing on a tour.

Admissions

Four main entry points — 11+ (Year 7), 13+ (Year 9), 14+ (Year 10) and 16+ (Sixth Form). The cycle is long and front-loaded. For 11+ 2028 entry: registration and exam form September 2027, assessment October 2027, offers November 2027, acceptance by March 2028.

Assessments combine adaptive online tests (Maths, English, Verbal Reasoning, and at 16+ subject-specific papers), group tasks and an interview. Overseas assessment centres run in Hong Kong (13+) and Singapore (16+). Non-native English speakers at 16+ must submit a UKiset profile.

Scholarships at 11+, 13+ and 16+ cover Academic, Music, Art and (from the next cycle) Drama — but scholarships carry no fee remission. Means-tested support is a separate programme (see Fees).

You need to start the process roughly 18 months before entry. If your daughter will be 11 in September 2028, you should be registering now. Overseas families: the HK and Singapore assessment centres mean you don't necessarily need to fly to the UK for the test.

Means-tested support: Beale Awards

The school's named bursary scheme. Here's what CLC publishes:

  • Award range: 1% to 100% of fees. Actual size determined by means-testing.
  • ~65 pupils on Beale Awards, total cost to the school ~£1.6M/year. That's about 7% of pupils.
  • Assessed on: (a) a means-testing review of family finances, AND (b) the daughter's performance in entrance tests and interviews.
  • 2027 entry timeline (Year 7): register by mid-September 2026 → assessments first two weeks October 2026 → financial review follows → decisions January 2027.
  • Continuation: awards are reviewed annually. CLC states "where a family's financial circumstances remain broadly the same, the level of support would normally be expected to continue at a similar level."

⚠️ Critical timing gotcha #1: Beale Awards are only open at 11+ and 16+ — NOT at 13+ or 14+. If you need bursary help and your daughter is targeting 13+ entry, you can't get one. Plan your entry year accordingly.

⚠️ Critical gap #2: CLC does not publish a household-income bracket that qualifies for meaningful support. "Means-tested" can mean anything. Before you register, ask Admissions directly: "What is the realistic household income ceiling at which a 50% award is possible?" A well-run bursary office will give you a concrete answer — a vague one is a signal.

Reassurance: the continuation language is strong. If your daughter gets in with 80% support at age 11, and your family situation doesn't materially change, she should finish with a similar award. That is as much certainty as any UK bursary offers.

Pastoral care & facilities

CLC runs twelve boarding houses split into Junior (11–16) and Senior (Sixth Form) provision, each led by a Houseparent with a deputy, residential pastoral assistants and academic tutors. Day girls use Eversleigh House (Junior) and Bayshill (Sixth Form) as their base, with early-evening wrap-around to 7pm.

Per the 2022 ISI report: 50 identified SEND pupils (15 receiving specialist help); ~one third of pupils are EAL, with 50 receiving additional English support. No pupil on roll at that inspection had an EHC plan.

"The quality of the pupils' personal development is excellent." — ISI inspection, November 2022

Daily life at CLC

What a day actually looks like — the things parents worry about at 2am.

🏠 Boarding structure

~160 girls join each year into an ~850-pupil community; the majority board. 12 houses total, split between Junior (Year 7–11) and Senior (Sixth Form).

Each house is run by a Houseparent and a deputy, supported by residential pastoral assistants and academic tutors. That's a dedicated adult rota — not a single overnight matron.

Source: CLC Boarding pages + ISI 2022.

📅 Weekends

Boarders may leave with a parent/guardian after Saturday commitments and return for supper Sunday evening.

First and last weekends of term are "closed weekends" — full attendance required. Around 20 sports teams play every Saturday, so sport is the default Saturday activity even for non-leavers. House events include shared meals, baking competitions, and movie nights.

What the school does NOT publish: what Sunday actually looks like for a boarder whose family can't collect her — e.g. most international boarders. Ask on tour: "Walk me through a typical Sunday for a Year 7 girl from Hong Kong who can't go home."

💚 Wellbeing & mental health — real staffing

CLC publishes its Mental Health Team structure on the Wellbeing page. The named roles are:

  • Head of Medical Health & Welfare (co-manages team with Head of Learning Support)
  • 3 Therapists — "targeted interventions"
  • 1 Emotional Health Support Nurse — low-level support, individuals or small groups
  • 1 Clinical Psychologist — complex cases + SpLD pre-screening

External: CAMHS, Nuffield Health, Health Security Agency, Physiotherapy Clinics. Plus weekly PSHE/Wellbeing programme, 24-hour medical centre, chaplaincy, "Well Place" space.

Rough ratio (our calculation): 5 named in-house clinically-facing roles across 862 pupils ≈ 1 : 172; 3 therapists alone ≈ 1 : 287. These are numbers we computed — CLC doesn't publish the ratio itself.

Still not in public data: average wait-time to see a counsellor, annual referral counts, severity mix. Where to find: ask for the Wellbeing Policy and Mental Health Policy (available on request), and ask the Director of Wellbeing directly at the tour.

Sector anchor (what "good" looks like): NHS England's CAMHS target is first contact within 4 weeks of referral; the actual 2022–23 national average was ~35 days, with some regions reporting 29+ week waits. Place2Be's standard school-counselling programme is 10 sessions of 50 minutes. A fee-paying school that can't answer "how quickly can my daughter see a counsellor?" is a flag — a good one will quote you days, not "it varies."

📱 Phone & device policy

CLC publishes an Online Safety Policy (2025–26) and an Acceptable Use Policy for Pupils (available on request). So there is a written framework — the school isn't relying on vibes.

The specific by-year-group rules (when phones are handed in, whether there's a night lock-up in boarding, WhatsApp group moderation) aren't extractable from the public PDF. Request a copy of the Online Safety Policy before you sign.

🍽️ Food & catering — source gap

CLC's boarding pages reference "shared meals" and the Wellbeing page mentions "freshly prepared food" in the medical centre. The CLC Venues & Events site describes external-event catering as "carefully curated, locally sourced, seasonal" — but does not name the catering partner.

Whether CLC is catered by Holroyd Howe, Thomas Franks, Chartwells, CH&Co, or operates in-house — is not published on the school's website or in our source data.

Where to find the real answer, in order of reliability:

  1. Ask on the tour — a single direct question: "Who caters the school, in-house or contracted?"
  2. Charity Commission accounts notes — the full trustee report usually lists major suppliers including catering contractor. Available via the Charity Commission site (charity 311722).
  3. LinkedIn — a search for "Catering Manager, Cheltenham Ladies' College" typically surfaces the employer (school or contractor) in the person's role description.
  4. Mumsnet boarding-school forums — food quality is discussed, though post freshness varies.
  5. Lunchtime visit during your tour — ask to eat in the dining hall, not just walk through it.

💛 Homesickness — first weeks

Houseparents + deputy + residential pastoral assistants + academic tutors form the frontline. The 24-hour medical centre covers physical welfare; chaplaincy and Well Place are positioned for emotional welfare.

CLC does not publish a specific first-fortnight settling-in protocol. Ask specifically: "What does the first two weeks look like for a Year 7 girl arriving from overseas, and who calls the parents if she's struggling?"

🛡️ Bullying policy

Three relevant documents at CLC, in order of accessibility:

  1. Behaviour, Discipline & Rewards Policy 2025–26 (public PDF, ~385 KB) — direct download. Defines bullying and escalation process.
  2. Anti-bullying & Anti-cyberbullying Policy — available on request only via CLC Contact Us. Notable that it isn't public.
  3. Online Safety Policy 2025–26 (public PDF) — covers WhatsApp, social-media and cyberbullying boundaries.

Honest note: we attempted to auto-extract CLC's bullying definition and escalation path from the Behaviour Policy PDF; the file isn't machine-readable via our current tooling. It's publicly downloadable at the link above — 2–3 minutes to skim.

What a typical UK indie-school bullying policy will commit to (orientation for what you're about to read, not a claim about CLC specifically): a definition covering physical/verbal/cyber/exclusion bullying; anonymous reporting channels (often a box, form, or named safeguarding lead); escalation through tutor → Houseparent → Deputy Head; parental notification within a stated timeframe; written record-keeping; and proportionate sanctions linked to the Behaviour Policy.

Annual incident counts are not publicly disclosed by any UK private school under any regulatory requirement — don't expect a number, but do expect a confident answer about process and a willingness to share the Anti-bullying Policy in writing.

🌙 Prep, sleep, supervision

Every boarding house has overnight adult coverage via the residential team (Houseparent/deputy/residential pastoral assistants).

Published prep hours and lights-out times by year group aren't in public materials. Ask your Houseparent directly: "What time is lights-out for a Year 7 in her first term?"

Honest assessment of this section: CLC is a transparent school about its structure (houseparent system, wellbeing framework, policy list) but less so about the specifics (exact phone rules, food feedback, homesickness protocol, prep schedule).

That's a sector-wide pattern, not a CLC-specific failing. What it means for you: treat the tour as your interview of the school — each "ask on tour" above is a litmus test. A well-run school gives confident, specific answers. A school that deflects is telling you something.

A specific tactic: request the Online Safety Policy, Anti-bullying Policy, and Mental Health Policy in advance of your tour. The school makes these available on request; they are the three documents that will answer most of your 2am questions.

  • Families who genuinely want British tradition done seriously, with current academic ambition at both A Level and IB.
  • Families who will use the co-curricular programme (140+ activities, Forum42, DofE, Saturday enrichment, global tours).
  • Girls who want a real bursary route at 11+ or 16+ — Beale Awards are meaningful and means-tested.
  • Internationally mobile families (HK, Singapore, Middle East, Africa) who value the overseas assessment centres.

Probably not the right school for

  • A child who wants a small, everyone-knows-everyone feel (~860 pupils).
  • Families wanting a co-educational environment.
  • Families wanting short day-school hours — the centre of gravity is full boarding, with Saturday school.
  • Families needing bursary help at 13+ or 14+ — Beale Awards are unavailable at those entry points.
  • A quieter child who needs active encouragement to speak up — the 2022 ISI recommendation about pupils raising matters is exactly the risk to probe there.

5 questions to ask on a tour

These are not gotcha questions. They are derived from the public record and phrased so you can hear the answer.

On the 2024 balance sheet jump "Total liabilities on the charity accounts rose from £15.83m (July 2023) to £32.49m (July 2024), alongside a £19m rise in total assets. What drove it — a specific capital project, new borrowing, or a pension/lease accounting change — and what is the school's net debt position today?"
On the one ISI recommendation "The November 2022 ISI report recommended ensuring pupils develop full confidence in their ability to raise matters of importance. What specific structures — pupil voice, complaints, tutor escalation — have changed since 2022, and how do you know they are working?"
On the leadership split "You have both an Executive Principal and a Head of College. Who makes the call on an individual girl's academic, pastoral and disciplinary issues, and who will my daughter's housemaster report to?"
On Oxbridge and destinations "You publish a combined 120 places at Oxbridge, Russell Group and Ivy League. How many girls went to Oxford and Cambridge specifically in 2024 and 2025, and what were their A-Level or IB profiles?"
On bursary progression "Beale Awards are available at 11+ and 16+ but not 13+ or 14+. If our daughter starts on a Beale Award at 11+, how is the award reviewed at key transitions, and what has your continuation rate looked like across the last five years?"

What would change this verdict

This isn't a snapshot you buy once and forget. We'd update this report immediately if any of the following happen:

  • A new ISI inspection report is published (one is overdue; expected 2025–26).
  • A Charity Commission serious incident report is filed.
  • Fees rise more than 8% year-on-year, or VAT on fees is passed through differently.
  • The Principal or Head of College changes, or a meaningful leadership change happens.
  • The school's net position declines for two consecutive years, or liabilities spike without a matching asset rise.
  • A new DfE TRA publication definitively ties a banned teacher to this school.

Glossary — every acronym in this report

If you're new to UK private-school jargon, here's a one-line definition of every acronym that appears above.

A-Level
UK exam taken at 18; three or four subjects in depth; the main path to UK university.
GCSE
General Certificate of Secondary Education — UK exam at 16, graded 9 (top) to 1.
IB
International Baccalaureate Diploma — alternative to A-Levels, scored out of 45; six subjects plus extended essay + theory of knowledge.
UCAS
Universities and Colleges Admissions Service — the central UK university application system.
ISI
Independent Schools Inspectorate — the government-approved body that inspects ISC-member private schools in England.
ISC
Independent Schools Council — the umbrella body for UK private-school associations (HMC, GSA, BSA etc.).
HMC / GSA / BSA
Headmasters' & Headmistresses' Conference / Girls' Schools Association / Boarding Schools' Association — peer associations that CLC sits within.
PSHE
Personal, Social, Health and Economic education — UK curriculum component covering life skills, mental health, relationships.
SEND
Special Educational Needs and Disabilities — pupils needing extra learning support.
EAL
English as an Additional Language — pupils whose first language isn't English.
EHC plan
Education, Health and Care plan — a legal document for children with significant additional needs; schools are obligated to deliver the support listed.
SpLD
Specific Learning Difficulties — umbrella term including dyslexia, ADHD, ASC (autism spectrum condition).
DfE
Department for Education — UK government department.
TRA
Teaching Regulation Agency — government body that handles teacher misconduct cases and maintains the prohibition list.
DofE
Duke of Edinburgh's Award — UK youth achievement programme (volunteering, physical, skill, expedition); Bronze/Silver/Gold.
LAMDA
London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art — graded speech-and-drama qualification; one-to-one lessons typically charged per term.
UKiset
UK Independent Schools Entry Test — online assessment of English, maths, and reasoning used for international applicants.
exeat
A short weekend break midway through term when boarders can leave school; CLC has one mid-term exeat per term.
SIR
Serious Incident Report — a filing charities must make to the Charity Commission when a safeguarding, financial, or reputational issue crosses a threshold.
CAMHS
Child and Adolescent Mental Health Services — NHS-based mental health provision for under-18s.
Russell Group
A self-selected group of 24 UK research-intensive universities (Oxford, Cambridge, Imperial, UCL, LSE, Durham, Edinburgh, KCL, Bristol, Manchester, etc.). Sometimes used as a proxy for "top UK university".
Royal Charter
A historical grant from the monarch giving an organisation legal status and governance framework, typically used by the oldest UK institutions.
