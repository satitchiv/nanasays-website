Reed's is set in Surrey's prosperous commuter belt — green, quiet, and 20 miles southwest of London. It's a genuine boarding-school setting: enough countryside to breathe, close enough to the capital that cultural trips, concerts, and airport runs don't take half a day.

Before you read the category breakdown, you need one piece of context that changes everything — especially if you're coming from Bangkok, Singapore, or Hong Kong:

What "violent crime" means in UK police data

In the United Kingdom, "violent crime" (officially "Violence Against the Person") captures any unwanted physical contact — including a push, a shove, a raised fist that didn't land, or verbal threats that escalated. Roughly 70–80% of these incidents involve no injury. In Thai, Singaporean, or Hong Kong police statistics, "violent crime" means serious assault, robbery with violence, or worse. The two definitions are not comparable. The number you see here is not the number you are imagining.
