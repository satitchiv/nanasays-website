Location & Getting Here

Reed's is in Surrey's commuter belt — 20 miles southwest of central London, green, quiet, and deliberately removed from city distraction. The school's immediate surroundings are prosperous suburbia: the kind of area where you can hear the M25 on a still morning but wouldn't know it from the school grounds.

Local Safety Context

The area around Reed's is safe — below the Surrey county average and well within normal range for a prosperous commuter suburb of its size.

Over the 12 months to January 2026, police recorded 208 incidents within one mile of the school. The number that will catch your eye is the 40% violent crime figure. Read this before you react:

What "violent crime" means in the UK — and why it's not what you're picturing. In UK police data, "Violence Against the Person" captures any unwanted physical contact — a push, a shove, verbal threats that escalate. Roughly 70–80% of incidents involve no injury. Thai, Singaporean, and Hong Kong statistics only count this category from serious assault upward. The two definitions are measuring different things. A parent from Bangkok reading "40% violent crime" in a UK report is looking at a statistic that has no equivalent in the crime data they're used to.
