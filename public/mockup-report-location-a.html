Location & Getting Here

Reed's sits in what estate agents call "affluent Surrey commuter country" — green, leafy, and quiet, about 20 miles southwest of central London. The nearest town is Cobham, a well-heeled commuter village of around 9,700 people, 2.7 km from the school gates. Think good coffee, a Waitrose, and not much else. For families who want their son somewhere calm and focused rather than caught up in city noise, this works well. For a child who craves urban energy on weekends, it's a significant trade-off.

The nearest train station is Oxshott, a 10-minute walk (0.9 km). From Oxshott you can reach London Waterloo in around 40 minutes — quick enough for a weekend exeat, slow enough that your son isn't slipping into London on a Tuesday evening.

For international families flying in, the airport picture is genuinely good:

When you visit: Windsor Castle is 14 km away — an easy half-day out for a family on an exeat weekend. Chessington World of Adventures is just 8 km if you have younger siblings in tow. Hampton Court Palace (12 km) and RHS Wisley Garden (7 km) round out a genuinely good weekend. The Surrey Hills are on the doorstep for walking families.

Local Safety Context

Below Surrey average

The area around Reed's is safe by UK and international standards. Over the 12 months to January 2026, police recorded 208 incidents within one mile of the school — a figure that sits at or below the Surrey county average of roughly 55–65 incidents per 1,000 residents per year.

If you're coming to this report from Bangkok, Singapore, or Hong Kong, the headline "40% violent crime" will likely jump out at you. Here's the context you need: in UK police data, "violent crime" includes any unwanted physical contact — a push, a raised hand, a verbal threat that turns physical. Roughly 70–80% of incidents in this category involve no injury whatsoever. It is not comparable to "violent crime" in Thai, Singaporean, or Hong Kong police statistics, where the equivalent category captures serious assault and above only. The gap between the definitions is substantial. If this school were in Bangkok, a significant portion of these 83 "violent crime" incidents would not appear in any crime statistic at all.

Breaking down the 208 incidents: anti-social behaviour (noise complaints, public rowdiness — 20%, 42 incidents) is routine suburban background noise. Burglary at 9% (19 incidents) is worth noting — Surrey's relative affluence does attract opportunistic property crime. Vehicle crime (5%) follows a similar pattern. Nothing in this distribution is unusual for a prosperous commuter area of this size.
