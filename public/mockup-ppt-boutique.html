Wycombe Abbey

A house among schools
EST. 1896 — Buckinghamshire, England — MAISON
— II — The Setting

A Wyatt house
upon a Buckingham
ridge.

Set within one hundred and seventy acres of Chiltern parkland, the school occupies a country house designed by James Wyatt in the late eighteenth century — once the seat of Lord Carrington.

The estate, with its lake and lime avenue, has been described as the most beautiful in English schooling. The pupils, in time, come to feel it.
