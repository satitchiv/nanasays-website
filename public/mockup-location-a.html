208 reported incidents within a mile over 12 months sits at or below the Surrey average for a commuter suburb. The headline "40% violent crime" is misleading without context — in UK police data, "violent crime" includes any unwanted physical contact, from a minor push to a serious assault. Roughly 70–80% of incidents in this category involve no injury. For families from Bangkok or Singapore where "violent crime" means serious assault only, this figure is not comparable and should not alarm.

How does this compare? England & Wales national average is ~85 incidents/1k residents/year. Surrey as a county runs 55–65 — one of the lowest in England. Bangkok's official rate is ~20–25/1k but is widely estimated to be 2–3× higher due to underreporting, with different category definitions. Singapore (~6–8/1k) and Hong Kong (~10–15/1k) use narrower violent crime definitions, so direct comparison overstates UK risk.
