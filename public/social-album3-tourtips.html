12 Questions
to Ask on
Your Tour
Most parents leave the school visit impressed but uninformed. Ask these instead.
1 / 8
NanaSays
Academics
1
What percentage of students achieve their target grades?
Why it matters: Marketing shows top scores. This question reveals what's normal — not exceptional.
2
Are students grouped by ability, or mixed in every class?
Why it matters: Streaming vs. mixed ability affects your child's daily experience and pace of learning far more than the curriculum name does.
2 / 8
NanaSays
Teachers
3
What is the average teacher tenure at this school?
Why it matters: High turnover means your child loses their teacher every year. Stability builds trust and continuity.
4
What professional development do teachers receive each year?
Why it matters: Schools that invest in their staff produce better outcomes. Ask for specifics — not just "ongoing training".
3 / 8
NanaSays
Fees & Costs
5
What costs are NOT included in the tuition fee quote?
Why it matters: Activity fees, uniforms, textbooks, and school trips can add 20–40% on top. Always ask for the full-year cost estimate.
6
By how much do fees typically increase each year?
Why it matters: A 5% annual rise means you pay significantly more by Year 6. Budget for the full journey, not just Year 1.
4 / 8
NanaSays
Student Wellbeing
7
How does the school handle bullying? Can you give a recent example?
Why it matters: Any school will say "we take it seriously." A real example tells you if the policy actually gets used — or just lives in a handbook.
8
What is the counsellor-to-student ratio?
Why it matters: Mental health support is scarce at most international schools. The ratio tells you whether your child can actually access help when they need it.
5 / 8
NanaSays
Admissions & Fit
9
What are the main reasons students leave before graduation?
Why it matters: Expat relocation is one answer. But "academic mismatch" or "pastoral issues" in the answer reveals real problems you'd never see on the tour.
10
What does the school value that a transcript doesn't show?
Why it matters: This exposes whether the school is a genuine fit for your child's personality — not just their grades.
6 / 8
NanaSays
University & Beyond
11
Where did your last three graduating classes actually go to university?
Why it matters: Schools highlight their best outcomes. Three years of real data shows the consistent picture — not just the outliers on the wall.
12
Which clubs and sports does the school genuinely invest in — not just list?
Why it matters: Resources follow real priorities. If the school has 30 clubs listed but only funds 5, your child may not get what they came for.
