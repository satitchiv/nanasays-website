What the competitions and rankings mean

🏆
Daily Mail Trophy
DMT · schoolsrugby.co.uk
Most important ranking

The definitive national merit table for school rugby in England. Schools' 1st XV results are fed into a points algorithm that accounts for opponent strength — so beating a highly-ranked school earns more merit points than beating a weaker one. 147–157 schools ranked each season. A top-30 rank means your school's 1st XV is among the best in the country. Run since 2013 in partnership with the Daily Mail.

🥇
Continental Tyres Schools Cup
RFU · Twickenham final
Most prestigious knockout cup

The RFU's national knockout cup for school rugby — the equivalent of an FA Cup for schools. Finals are played at Allianz Stadium, Twickenham. Reaching the final or winning is a strong signal of elite-level rugby. Previously known as the Daily Mail Cup and NatWest Schools Cup. Epsom College reached the 2026 final (lost 20–36 to Northampton School for Boys).

📊
SOCS Performance League
SOCS · Boys U18A
Useful secondary signal

A merit ranking run by SOCS (the school sports results platform used by most UK independent schools). Covers 300–340 schools — broader than DMT. Useful for spotting schools that are improving or that DMT hasn't picked up yet. A top-50 SOCS Performance rank alongside a mid-DMT rank often means a school is on the rise. Wells Cathedral's current #7 SOCS Performance rank (2025-26) is a flag worth watching.

📈
SOCS All Comers League
SOCS · Boys U18A
Broadest picture

The widest ranking — 400–550 schools including state schools and lower-division independents. Because it includes state schools (which can be very strong), rankings here are harder to interpret directly, but a top-10 rank is a genuinely outstanding signal. Millfield's #2 All Comers rank in 2024-25 and #7 this season confirms their elite status extends well beyond the independent school bubble.
