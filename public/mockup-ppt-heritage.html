Section II — Heritage

A House of
Old Standing

Founded in the closing years of the nineteenth century, Wycombe Abbey was conceived not as a school in the Victorian mould, but as something rarer: a house of learning where young women might be cultivated with the same gravity afforded their brothers at Eton and Harrow.

Frances Dove, its first headmistress, brought from Cheltenham a quiet certainty that has never quite left the place.

The estate itself — a Wyatt house on a Buckinghamshire ridge — was once the seat of Lord Carrington. Its corridors carry the weight of a former century without performing the part.

Today, six hundred and forty-two pupils pass through these doors. Not all will be remembered, but each is shaped by the rule of the place.
