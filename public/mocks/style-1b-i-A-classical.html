What are Reed's fees?

❦   ❦   ❦

Reed's day fees are £8,820 a term. Boarding is £14,200. The published numbers tell only part of the story — here is what they really cost a family at Reed's.

I. The Headline

For a parent comparing Reed's to other UK independent schools, the published 2025/26 fee schedule is the obvious starting point — but it is also a starting point that can mislead. Day pupils pay £8,820 a term; full boarders pay £14,200. Multiply by three terms and a school year lands at £26,460 for day, £42,600 for boarding.

Those numbers are real. They are also incomplete. Most parents end up adding ten to fifteen per cent on top once trips, music lessons, exam fees, and uniform are factored in. The honest "all-in" range is closer to £29-32k for a day pupil and £45-49k for a boarder.

Reed's sits at the lower-mid end of UK independent boarding costs, but the sibling discount has a sharper edge than most schools'.

III. A Note Worth Heeding
Reed's sibling discount applies from the third child onwards — not the second. If you have two children at Reed's, you do not get a discount. This is sharper than the typical UK independent, where the discount usually starts with the second child.
IV. The Gaps

Two notable absences from Reed's public materials. The 2026/27 fee uplift has not yet been published, and the bursary income thresholds — the cutoffs that determine partial fee remission — are not disclosed publicly. Both are worth raising directly with the bursar.
