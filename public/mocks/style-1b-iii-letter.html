What are Reed's fees?

The short version: Reed's day fees are £8,820 per term, and full boarding is £14,200 per term. Multiply through the school year and you get £26,460 for a day pupil and £42,600 for a boarder1 — before extras.

  • Day pupils, Y7–Y13: £8,820/term
  • Full boarding, Y9–Y13: £14,200/term
  • Registration £150, Deposit £2,000 on offer2

The headline numbers tell only part of the story. Most parents end up adding 10 to 15 percent on top once trips, music lessons, exam fees, and uniform are accounted for. Realistically you'll see about £29-32k/year for a day pupil and £45-49k for a boarder. For context, the ISC's 2024 average for UK independent boarding is around £42k — Reed's sits at the lower-mid end.

Reed's sibling discount applies from the third child onwards — not the second. If you have two children at Reed's, you don't get a discount. This is sharper than most UK independents, where the discount usually starts at the second child. Worth raising with the bursar if it matters to you.

Two things I couldn't confirm: the 2026/27 fee uplift hasn't been published yet, and Reed's doesn't publicly list its bursary income thresholds — the cutoffs that determine partial fee remission. Both are reasonable questions for an admissions or bursar conversation.
